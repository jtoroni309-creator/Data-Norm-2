# Management Representation Letter
## Aura Holdings Inc. and Subsidiaries
## For the Year Ended December 31, 2025

---

[Date: February 28, 2026]

[External Audit Firm]
[Address]

Dear Audit Partner:

This representation letter is provided in connection with your audit of the consolidated financial statements of Aura Holdings Inc. and Subsidiaries ("the Company") as of and for the year ended December 31, 2025, for the purpose of expressing an opinion as to whether the consolidated financial statements present fairly, in all material respects, the financial position, results of operations, and cash flows of Aura Holdings Inc. and Subsidiaries in conformity with accounting principles generally accepted in the United States of America (U.S. GAAP).

We confirm that we are responsible for the fair presentation of the consolidated financial statements in conformity with U.S. GAAP.

---

## GENERAL REPRESENTATIONS

We confirm, to the best of our knowledge and belief, as of February 28, 2026:

### Financial Statements

1. The consolidated financial statements referred to above are fairly presented in conformity with U.S. GAAP and include all disclosures necessary for such fair presentation and required by the rules and regulations of the Securities and Exchange Commission.

2. We have made available to you all:
   - Financial records and related data
   - Minutes of meetings of stockholders, directors, and committees of directors
   - Contracts, agreements, and documentation significant to the financial statements

3. There are no material transactions that have not been properly recorded in the accounting records underlying the financial statements.

4. We acknowledge our responsibility for the design, implementation, and maintenance of internal control relevant to the preparation and fair presentation of financial statements that are free from material misstatement, whether due to fraud or error.

---

### Fraud and Illegal Acts

5. We have no knowledge of:
   - Fraud or suspected fraud involving management or employees with significant roles in internal control
   - Fraud or suspected fraud involving others that could have a material effect on the financial statements
   - Allegations of fraud or suspected fraud affecting the Company received from employees, former employees, analysts, regulators, or others

6. We have no knowledge of any actual, suspected, or alleged noncompliance with laws and regulations whose effects should be considered for disclosure in the financial statements or as a basis for recording a contingent liability.

---

### Related Parties

7. Related party relationships and transactions have been appropriately accounted for and disclosed in accordance with the requirements of U.S. GAAP. Related parties are listed in Schedule A attached.

8. There are no side agreements or other arrangements (either written or oral) with related parties.

---

### Accounting Estimates

9. Significant assumptions used in making accounting estimates, including those measured at fair value, are reasonable. Specifically:
   - Allowance for doubtful accounts: Based on historical loss experience and current conditions
   - Inventory obsolescence reserves: Based on aging analysis and sales forecasts
   - Goodwill impairment assessments: Based on discounted cash flow analysis
   - Lease liability discount rates: Based on incremental borrowing rates
   - Contingency accruals: Based on probability-weighted expected outcomes

---

### Subsequent Events

10. All events subsequent to the date of the financial statements and for which U.S. GAAP requires adjustment or disclosure have been adjusted or disclosed. Subsequent events through the date of this letter include:
    - Collection of $49.3M of accounts receivable per schedule
    - Settlement of Industrial Components Corp. litigation for $525,000 (February 15, 2026)
    - No other significant subsequent events requiring disclosure

---

## SPECIFIC REPRESENTATIONS

### Revenue Recognition (ASC 606)

11. Revenue has been recognized in accordance with ASC 606, including:
    - Identification of performance obligations
    - Determination of transaction prices
    - Allocation of transaction prices to performance obligations
    - Recognition of revenue when/as performance obligations are satisfied

12. Contract modifications have been properly accounted for.

13. Costs to obtain and fulfill contracts have been properly capitalized and amortized (Sub C: $6.5M deferred commission costs).

---

### Leases (ASC 842)

14. All lease arrangements have been properly identified and classified as operating or finance leases in accordance with ASC 842.

15. Lease terms include reasonably certain renewal and termination options.

16. Incremental borrowing rates used are appropriate for our credit standing and lease terms.

17. Short-term lease exemptions have been appropriately applied.

---

### Consolidation and Foreign Currency

18. All entities requiring consolidation have been included.

19. The Company owns 100% of Sub A, 80% of Sub B, and 60% of Sub C.

20. Non-controlling interests have been appropriately measured and presented.

21. Foreign currency translation has been performed using appropriate rates:
    - Sub B functional currency: EUR
    - Year-end rate: 1.08 EUR/USD
    - Average rate: 1.085 EUR/USD

22. Intercompany transactions and balances have been appropriately eliminated.

---

### Income Taxes

23. We have properly identified and evaluated uncertain tax positions in accordance with ASC 740.

24. The deferred tax asset valuation allowance is adequate.

25. We have evaluated all tax positions and believe they are more-likely-than-not to be sustained.

---

### Commitments and Contingencies

26. All known actual or possible litigation, claims, and assessments have been disclosed to you and are:
    - Properly accounted for in accordance with ASC 450
    - Appropriately accrued or disclosed in the financial statements

27. The Company has complied with all aspects of contractual agreements that could have a material effect on the financial statements in the event of noncompliance.
